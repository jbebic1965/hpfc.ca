The Symmetrical
Hybrid Power Flow Controller

A new technology for Flexible AC Transmission (FACTS)

This site's mission

Our mission is to promote the concept of HPFC to research community worldwide.

How did the concept of HPFC come about?

The HPFC was conceived at University of Toronto by Dr. Jovan Bebic, then a Ph.D. student, and his thesis supervisor Dr. Peter Lehn. The thesis, titled "A Symmetrical Hybrid power Flow Controller", was defended in Sep. 2003.

Jovan's thesis advisors, Drs. Peter Lehn and Reza Iravani, provided invaluable guidance and support over the years. In particular, Jovan's interest in this field and the inspiration for much of the work came from being Dr. Iravani's graduate student. Furthermore, Dr. Lehn worked very closely with Jovan, persevering through the toughest of problems, and sharing in the joy of every solution. Notably, Dr. Lehn proposed the IM-SM based configuration and the dual configuration suitable for retrofitting series capacitors.
